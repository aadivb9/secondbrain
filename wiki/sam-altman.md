## How To Be Successful — 13 Compounding Principles

The most-cited piece. A complete system for high-agency life:

| # | Principle | Operating Rule |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Compound Yourself** | Pursue exponential, not linear, growth. Long-term thinking is competitive advantage. |
| 2 | **Have Almost Too Much Self-Belief** | Believe when others doubt — balanced with openness to criticism. |
| 3 | **Think Independently** | First-principles reasoning. "I will fail many times, and I will be really right once." |

## Startup Playbook — Operating Manual

### The Four Essentials

Great idea + great team + great product + great execution.

### Idea
- Best ideas sound bad but are good
- Target large portion of small market > small portion of large market
- Look for emerging tech shifts most haven't seen
- 10x better, never derivative

### Team
- Founder traits: unstoppable, formidable, intelligent, low-stress, rigid+flexible, unusually responsive, clear communicator
- **Cofounder breakups = #1 cause of early death**
- Choose someone you know well (never matching events)
- Best→worst: good cofounder → solo → bad cofounder

### Product
- "The only thing all great companies have in common"
- Talk to users constantly. Get physically close.
- "Do things that don't scale" mantra (→ [[paul-graham]])
- 5% weekly improvement compounds dramatically
- Launch sooner than intuition suggests

### Growth
- **"Never lose momentum" = prime directive**
- Single growth metric posted everywhere (Airbnb: graphs on fridge, mirrors)
- Founder burnout almost always = no momentum
- Avoid: premature scaling worry, growth theater, deals/PR

### CEO's 5 Jobs
1. Set vision/strategy
2. Evangelize
3. Hire/manage (esp. gaps)
4. Raise money
5. Set quality bar

### Hiring
- Wait long. Employees expensive + add inertia.
- 25% of CEO time on recruiting from PMF onward
- Don't compromise on people quality. **Trust your gut. Doubt = no.**
- Don't hire chronically negative people. Aptitude > experience.
- "Don't go into hero mode" — top first-time-manager mistake
- Fire quickly. Fire toxic regardless of skill.

### Competitors
> "99% die from suicide, not murder."

Ignore competitors 99% of the time.

### Fundraising
- "Secret to raising money is to have a good company"
- Parallel, not sequential
- Believe the no, not the reason
- Insist on clean terms
- Get to **ramen profitability** ASAP — control your destiny

## Idea Generation

- Surround yourself with high-idea-flux, optimistic people
- Project 20 years forward, work backward
- Identify tectonic shifts (mobile 2008–2012)
- "Anything possible this year that wasn't last year — pay attention"
- Founder-company alignment matters as much as product-market fit
- Strong idea: you can articulate why most think it's bad, but you understand why it's good

## Moore's Law for Everything (2021)

Macro thesis on AI's economic transformation:
- AI will reshape economies by reducing labor costs across supply chains
- 5 years: AI handles legal review, medical diagnosis
- Decades: "almost everything, including new scientific discoveries"
- **Wealth problem:** redistribution alone fails if costs of essentials soar
- **Solution:** make goods/services exponentially cheaper via AI = real abundance

**American Equity Fund proposal:**
- 2.5% annual tax on company market value (paid in shares)
- 2.5% wealth tax on land
- Distribute as universal dividend → ~$13.5K/adult/year within a decade
