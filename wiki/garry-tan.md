## Who He Is

CEO of Y Combinator (since 2023). Former designer at Posterous, co-founder of Initialized Capital ($7M Fund I returned 11–14X via Coinbase, Instacart). Joined YC in 2011 — bridges PG-era YC with the AI-native era. Most influential public-facing voice on early-stage startups today.

## Core Theses

### The AI Earthquake

> "There was an earthquake, and people are acting like it didn't happen."

- Winter 2025 batch averaged **10% week-over-week growth**
- $10M revenue with teams under 10 people
- ~25% of YC startups have **95% AI-written code**
- Companies hitting $10–20M ARR in 10–20 months

**Implication:** The unit economics of starting a company changed. Vibe coding removed technical barriers — success now depends on **understanding customer problems** and **taste**.

### Speed as Moat

> "Ship early, instrument everything, and assume your product will be obsolete in three months — unless you obsolete it first."

In an AI-built world, the only defensible advantage is the rate at which you obsolete yourself.

### Earnestness Filter

The single quality Tan talks about most: **earnestness** — a genuine, almost naive belief in what you're building, plus willingness to do the work that belief requires. Separates founders who build lasting companies from those who build for exit.

### Ownership > Fund Size

Lesson from Initialized: percentage stakes matter more than fund size. Fund II was 5X larger but didn't 5X check sizes — diluted ownership reduced returns despite producing multiple unicorns. **Concentration > diversification at the seed stage.**

## What Garry Looks For in Founders

| Trait | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **High agency** | Overcome obstacles through willpower + creative problem-solving. Develops, not innate. |
| **Beginner's mind** | First-time founders lack biases. Experienced founders must consciously relearn. |
| **Customer intimacy** | Deep pain understanding via lived experience or immersion |
| **Taste/discernment** | Evaluate what actually works across contexts |
| **Earnestness** | Genuine belief + willingness to schlep |

## Practical Advice for AI-Era Founders

1. **Go undercover** — work in your target industry to develop empathy (e.g., spend a week as a medical biller to understand workflows)
2. **Do unscalable things** — skip perfect systems initially; ship + learn
3. **Combine skills strategically** — pair coding with domain expertise
4. **Pivot toward known territory** — move from "should do" ideas toward authentic insight
5. **Hire people smarter than yourself** — relentlessly
6. **"Why now?"** — timing insight that makes the market viable

## Growth Metrics That Matter (per Garry)

- **Weekly revenue growth** (10% w/w benchmark)
- Retention rates + gross margins
- **Customer pull** (do they demand it without persuasion?)
- Ignore: vanity signups, capital-driven scaling, press
