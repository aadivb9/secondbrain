# Philosophy

> "The unexamined life is not worth living." — Socrates

Love of wisdom. Systematic inquiry into existence, knowledge, morality, reason, and mind. Every other field is downstream of philosophy.

---

## The 5 Core Branches

| Branch | One-liner | Key question |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Epistemology** | Theory of knowledge | How do you know what you know? |
| **Metaphysics** | Theory of reality | What actually exists? |
| **Ethics** | Theory of morality | What should you do? |
| **Logic** | Theory of reasoning | What makes an argument valid? |
| **Aesthetics** | Theory of beauty/art | What is beauty? Is it objective? |

Sub-fields: philosophy of mind, philosophy of science, political philosophy, philosophy of language.

## The Big Debates

### Free Will vs Determinism
Three positions:
1. **Hard Determinism** — everything is caused; free will is illusion (Laplace, Spinoza)
2. **Compatibilism** — free will = acting from your own desires, even if determined (Dennett, Frankfurt, Kant)
3. **Libertarian Free Will** — genuine agent causation exists; quantum indeterminacy leaves room

Neuroscience wrinkle: Libet experiments show brain activity precedes conscious decision by ~300ms — consciousness may be post-hoc narration.

### Mind-Body Problem
- **Substance Dualism** (Descartes) — mind and body are separate substances. Problem: how do they interact?
- **Physicalism** — mind = brain; consciousness is physical. Problem: qualia (the redness of red)
- **Functionalism** — mental states = functional roles; substrate-independent (important for AI)
- **Panpsychism** — consciousness is fundamental; everything has some form of experience (Chalmers)
- **The Hard Problem** (Chalmers) — explaining *why* physical processes produce subjective experience at all

### Epistemology: Rationalism vs Empiricism
- **Rationalism**: Innate ideas; reason alone → necessary truths (math, logic). Descartes, Leibniz.
- **Empiricism**: Blank slate; all ideas from sensory experience. Locke, Hume.
- **Kant's synthesis**: Both — mind has innate *categories* (space, time, causality) that structure raw sensory input.

### Ethics: Three Frameworks
| Framework | Core | Flaw |
|-----------|------|------|
| **Deontology** (Kant) | Follow duty/rules regardless of outcomes | Can produce bad outcomes |
| **Consequentialism** (Mill) | Maximize good outcomes | Justifies monstrous acts if outcome is good |
| **Virtue Ethics** (Aristotle) | Cultivate character traits of a good person | Vague on what to do in specific cases |

## Philosophy of Mind — Fastest Download

The field asking: what IS consciousness, and how does it relate to the physical?

- **Qualia** — the subjective "what it's like" to see red, taste sugar, feel pain
- **Intentionality** — mental states are *about* things; they have content
- **The Hard Problem** — why does any physical process produce experience at all?
- **Chinese Room** (Searle) — syntax (symbol manipulation) ≠ semantics (meaning/understanding). Direct challenge to strong AI.
- **Multiple Realizability** — same mental state can be implemented in different physical substrates (supports functionalism → supports AI consciousness)
- **Zombie thought experiment** — could there be a being physically identical to you but with no inner experience? If yes → physicalism is false.

Contrarian take: most "productivity" and "mindset" content is watered-down philosophy. Read the primary sources — Meditations (Marcus), Enchiridion (Epictetus), Nausea (Sartre) — you'll get 10x the signal.

## Starter Reading List (Ranked by Signal/Effort)

1. **Meditations** — Marcus Aurelius (Stoic, practical, 1 hour)
2. **Enchiridion** — Epictetus (Stoic, short, dense)
3. **The Stranger** — Camus (existentialism via fiction)
4. **Sophie's World** — Jostein Gaarder (full history of philosophy as a novel)
5. **The Problems of Philosophy** — Bertrand Russell (analytic intro, 150 pages)
6. **Justice** — Michael Sandel (ethics through real cases, Harvard lectures on YouTube)
7. **Gödel, Escher, Bach** — Hofstadter (logic, mind, self-reference, consciousness)
