# Philosophy — Raw Source: Overview

## Definition
Philosophy (Greek: *philosophia* = "love of wisdom") — systematic study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, mind, reason, language, and value. Both a field and an act: whenever you reason critically and argue carefully, you philosophize.

## Core Branches

| Branch | Studies | Key Questions |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Epistemology** | Knowledge | What is knowledge? Can we trust perception? Is knowledge innate or acquired? What are its limits? |
| **Metaphysics** | Reality | What exists? What is consciousness? Do we have free will? Why does anything exist at all? |
| **Ethics** | Morality | What is right/wrong? What do we owe each other? How do we live well? |
| **Logic** | Reasoning | What makes an argument valid? How do we avoid fallacies? |
| **Aesthetics** | Beauty/Art | Is beauty objective? What makes art meaningful? |
| **Philosophy of Mind** | Consciousness | What is the mind? Can matter think? Is there a soul? |
| **Philosophy of Science** | Scientific method | What is a scientific explanation? What distinguishes science from non-science? |
| **Political Philosophy** | Society | What justifies power? What is justice? What rights do individuals have? |

## Major Schools of Thought

### Ancient (600 BCE – 400 CE)
- **Pre-Socratics** — Thales, Heraclitus, Parmenides; first naturalistic explanations (no gods)
- **Socratic** — Socrates: "know thyself"; the examined life; the Socratic method
- **Platonism** — Plato: Forms (ideal abstractions are more real than physical things); philosopher-kings
- **Aristotelianism** — Aristotle: empiricism, logic, virtue ethics, teleology; "man is a rational animal"
- **Stoicism** — Zeno of Citium, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, Seneca; virtue = highest good; control what you can; amor fati
- **Epicureanism** — Epicurus; pleasure (ataraxia) = freedom from anxiety; simple life; death is nothing to fear
- **Skepticism** — Pyrrho; suspend judgment; appearances only; no certainty possible
- **Cynicism** — Diogenes; reject social convention; live according to nature

### Medieval (400–1400)
- **Scholasticism** — Aquinas, Anselm; reason + faith; reconcile Aristotle with Christianity
- **Islamic Philosophy** — Avicenna, Averroes; preserved Greek thought; added reason/revelation synthesis

### Early Modern (1600–1800)
- **Rationalism** — Descartes, Leibniz, Spinoza; reason alone yields knowledge; *cogito ergo sum*
- **Empiricism** — Locke, Berkeley, Hume; all knowledge from sensory experience; tabula rasa
- **Kantian** — Kant; synthetic a priori; categories of mind structure experience; duty-based ethics (deontology)
- **Social Contract** — Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau; political authority justified by consent; natural rights

### 19th Century
- **Utilitarianism** — Bentham, Mill; maximize happiness for the greatest number; consequentialism
- **German Idealism** — Hegel; Absolute Spirit; dialectic (thesis → antithesis → synthesis); history has direction
- **Marxism** — Marx; material conditions drive history; class struggle; alienation of labor
- **Nietzsche** — God is dead; will to power; Übermensch; master vs. slave morality; eternal recurrence; perspectivism

### 20th Century
- **Existentialism** — Kierkegaard (precursor), Heidegger, Sartre, Camus, de Beauvoir; existence precedes essence; radical freedom; authenticity; absurdity
- **Phenomenology** — Husserl, Heidegger; study consciousness and lived experience directly; bracketing assumptions
- **Analytic Philosophy** — Russell, Wittgenstein, Frege; logic + language analysis; clarity; "What does this sentence mean?"
- **Pragmatism** — Pierce, James, Dewey (American); truth = what works; ideas are tools; action over abstraction
- **Logical Positivism** — Vienna Circle; only empirically verifiable or logically necessary claims are meaningful
- **Continental Philosophy** — Sartre, Foucault, Derrida, Deleuze; power, language, society, deconstruction
- **Structuralism / Post-Structuralism** — Saussure, Derrida; language structures reality; meaning is relational/unstable

### Contemporary
- **Analytic metaphysics** — modal logic, possible worlds (Kripke, Lewis)
- **Philosophy of mind** — functionalism, qualia, the hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers)
- **Applied ethics** — bioethics, AI ethics, environmental ethics, global justice
- **Free will debates** — Compatibilism (Dennett, Frankfurt) vs. Hard Determinism vs. Libertarianism (agent causation)

## Key Debates You Need to Know

| Debate | Positions |
|--------|-----------|
| **Free will vs determinism** | Hard determinism / Compatibilism / Libertarian free will |
| **Mind-body problem** | Dualism (Descartes) / Physicalism / Functionalism / Panpsychism |
| **Moral realism** | Moral facts exist objectively vs. constructed vs. subjective |
| **Epistemology** | Rationalism vs. Empiricism vs. Kant's synthesis |
| **God's existence** | Cosmological, ontological, teleological arguments vs. Problem of evil |
| **Personal identity** | What makes you "you" over time? (Locke's memory, Hume's bundle theory) |

## Eastern Philosophy (Brief)
- **Indian** — Vedanta, Buddhism (dukkha, impermanence, no-self), Jainism, Nyaya (logic), Yoga
- **Chinese** — Confucianism (social harmony, filial piety), Taoism (wu wei, flow), Legalism, Chan Buddhism
- **Buddhist logic** — Nagarjuna's Madhyamaka; emptiness (śūnyatā); middle way

## Why Philosophy Matters for a Builder

- **Epistemology** → how you evaluate claims, spot BS, design experiments
- **Ethics** → how you make product decisions, what you won't build
- **Logic** → how you argue, debug thinking, avoid cognitive biases
- **Philosophy of mind** → foundational to understanding AI and consciousness
- **Political philosophy** → how you think about power, governance, institutions
- Virtually every field has a "philosophy of X" — understanding it accelerates mastery
