## How To Be Successful — 13 Points

1. **Compound Yourself.** Exponential growth in career. Pursue compounding opportunities; long-term thinking as competitive advantage. Build leverage via capital, technology, brand, networks.
2. **Have Almost Too Much Self-Belief.** Believe in yourself when others doubt. Balance with self-awareness/openness to criticism. Sustains morale through challenges that defeat others.
3. **Learn to Think Independently.** First-principles reasoning. Test ideas quickly in real world. "I will fail many times, and I will be really right once." Grit develops by recovering from setbacks.
4. **Get Good at "Sales".** All significant careers involve persuasion. Clear thinking precedes clear communication. Genuine belief in product makes it feel meaningful. Show up in person.
5. **Make It Easy to Take Risks.** Most overestimate risk, underestimate reward. Asymmetric bets: lose 1x if wrong, make 100x if right. Avoid comfortable salary trap.
6. **Focus.** "Much more important to work on the right thing than to work many hours." Speed matters. Strategic focus multiplies effort.
7. **Work Hard.** 90th percentile = intelligence OR effort; 99th percentile = both. Find work + people you respect to sustain effort. Start intense early.
8. **Be Bold.** Ambitious work attracts collaborators. Pursue genuinely important problems. "If everyone else is starting meme companies, and you want to start a gene-editing company, then do that."
9. **Be Willful.** "You can bend the world to your will a surprising percentage of the time." Most accept things as fixed. Ask for what you want despite likely rejection.
10. **Be Hard to Compete With.** Distinctive capabilities. Personal relationships, brand, unique skill combos. Avoid mimetic behavior — doing what everyone does = commoditization.
11. **Build a Network.** "Great work requires teams." Sustained helpfulness. Generous upside-sharing. Discover undervalued talent. Hire complementary teammates.
12. **You Get Rich by Owning Things.** "Almost no one in the history of the Forbes list has gotten there with a salary." Equity, real estate, IP. Time-based income scales linearly; ownership exponentially.
13. **Be Internally Driven.** Internally motivated people pursue personally meaningful work. Prevents consensus thinking. After financial security, internal motivation is the only sustainable driver.

## Startup Playbook (key tactics)

**4 essentials:** great idea (incl. market) + great team + great product + great execution.

**Idea:**
- Best ideas sound bad but are good
- Target large portion of small market > small portion of large market
- 10x better, not derivative
- Look for emerging technological shifts most haven't recognized

**Team:**
- Founder traits: unstoppability, formidability, intelligence, low-stress, rigid+flexible, unusually responsive, clear communicator
- Cofounder breakups = #1 cause of early death
- Choose someone you know well — never matching events
- Best→worst: good cofounder → solo → bad cofounder

**Product:**
- "The only thing all great companies have in common"
- Talk to users constantly. Get physically close.
- "Do things that don't scale" mantra
- 5% weekly improvement compounds dramatically
- Launch sooner than intuition suggests; simplicity wins
- Indicators: users return + fanatical + would be upset if gone + unprompted referrals

**Execution / Growth:**
- "Never lose momentum" = prime directive
- Single growth metric posted everywhere (Airbnb posted graphs on fridge, mirrors)
- Founder burnout almost always correlates with no momentum
- Avoid: premature scaling worry, technical debt obsession, growth theater (deals/PR)

**CEO 5 jobs:**
1. Set vision/strategy
2. Evangelize
3. Hire/manage (especially gaps)
4. Raise money
5. Set execution quality bar

**Hiring:**
- Wait long. Employees are expensive + add inertia.
- 25% of CEO time on recruiting from PMF onward
- Don't compromise on people quality. Trust gut. Doubt = no.
- Don't hire chronically negative people
- Aptitude > experience
- "Don't go into hero mode" — most common new-manager mistake
- Fire quickly. Fire toxic people regardless of skill.

**Competitors:** "99% die from suicide, not murder." Ignore competitors 99% of time.

**Fundraising:**
- "Secret to successfully raising money is to have a good company"
- Raise in parallel, not sequential
- Believe the no, not the reason
- Insist on clean terms; don't over-optimize valuation
- Get to ramen profitability ASAP — control your destiny

## Moore's Law for Everything

- AI will fundamentally reshape economies by reducing labor costs
- "Software that can think and learn will do more and more of the work people now do"
- Within 5 years: AI handles legal review, medical diagnosis
- Within decades: "almost everything, including new scientific discoveries"
- Disruption is "unstoppable"

**Wealth creation problem:** Tech progress alone won't ensure prosperity. Redistribution alone won't work if costs (housing, healthcare) keep soaring.

**American Equity Fund proposal:**
- 2.5% annual tax on company market value (paid in shares)
- 2.5% wealth tax on privately-held land
- Distribute as dividend to all adults
- Projection: ~$13,500/adult annually within decade

## Idea Generation

- "Giving founders an idea almost always doesn't work"
- Surround yourself with people with high idea flux + optimism + willingness to entertain improbable plans
- Project 20 years forward, work backward
- Identify tectonic shifts (mobile 2008–2012 example)
- "Any time you can think of something that is possible this year and wasn't possible last year, you should pay attention"
- Founder-company alignment matters as much as product-market fit
- A strong idea: you can articulate why most people think it's bad, but you understand what makes it good
