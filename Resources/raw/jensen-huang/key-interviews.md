## Accelerated Computing Philosophy

> "Nvidia is fundamentally an accelerated computing company, not a GPU company."

- Goal isn't eliminating CPUs but optimizing entire applications across heterogeneous systems
- General computing era is ending — specialized accelerated systems take over

## The Five-Layer Cake

AI infrastructure as 5 interconnected layers:
1. Power
2. Chips
3. Infrastructure
4. Models
5. Applications

> "We have to win each layer independently rather than bundling them vertically."

> "It's a terrible mistake to think that the way to win is to bundle all of it top-to-bottom and tie every company together into one holistic stack."

Anti-bundling → strengthens American technological leadership.

## Extreme Co-Design

> "We create a thing vertically and then we open it horizontally so everybody could use whatever piece they would like."

- Vertical optimization, then horizontal distribution
- Innovate + optimize simultaneously across algorithms, models, systems, software, networks, chips

## AI Factory Economics

- Gigawatt facility costs **$50–60 billion**
- ~$15–17B in land, power, infrastructure
- Predicted **$1 trillion in orders by 2027** (GTC)

## Strategic Positioning

> "Nvidia is not just a chip company but an AI infrastructure partner."
> Building competitive barriers through "Extreme Co-Design"

## On AI's Impact on Work

> "PCs made us more busy, the internet made us more busy, mobile devices made us super busy… AI is going to get tasks done super fast… my sense is that AI is going to cause us to be able to do things so fast we're going to end up doing more."

## Leadership Style

- Discredits "stick to your knitting" advice — Nvidia survived because Jensen pivoted toward CUDA + accelerated computing decades before payoff
- Patience for long-term bets when the underlying tech curve is right
- Direct, technical communication — no buffers
