## AI-Built Startups Thesis (Vanta interview)

**Core thesis:** AI fundamentally changes startup dynamics. Recent YC batches achieve $8M-$12M ARR within 9-12 months with under 10 people — previously impossible. "Vibe coding" removes technical barriers, shifting success factors from execution capability to **understanding customer problems and building solutions people actually want**.

**Growth metrics he prioritizes:**
- Weekly revenue growth (10% week-over-week as benchmark)
- Retention rates and gross margins
- Customer pull signals (when customers demand the product without persuasion)
- Weighting machines over popularity contests — real business fundamentals matter more than capital-driven scaling

**What he looks for in founders:**
- **High agency**: ability to overcome obstacles through willpower and creative problem-solving (develops progressively, not innate)
- **Beginner's mind**: first-time founders lack biases; experienced founders must consciously relearn
- **Intimacy with customer problems**: founders should deeply understand user pain through lived experience or immersion
- **Taste and discernment**: evaluating what actually works across scenarios
- **Earnestness**: a genuine, almost naive belief in what they're building, and the willingness to do the work that belief requires

**Advice for early-stage AI companies:**
1. **Go undercover** — work in your target industry to develop genuine empathy (e.g., medical biller role to understand workflows)
2. **Do unscalable things** — skip perfect systems initially; prioritize shipping and learning
3. **Combine skills strategically** — pair strong coding ability with domain expertise for competitive advantage
4. **Pivot toward known territory** — move from "should do" ideas toward spaces where founders have authentic insight

## "AI Earthquake" Quotes (CNBC)

> "There was an earthquake, and people are acting like it didn't happen."

- Winter 2025 batch grew avg **10% per week**
- Some hitting $10M revenue with teams under 10
- For ~25% of current YC startups, **95% of code was AI-written**
- YC companies with 10–20 people getting to $10–20M ARR in 10–20 months

**Speed thesis:**
> "Ship early, instrument everything, and assume your product will be obsolete in three months — unless you obsolete it first."

## YC Career & Initialized (Acquired podcast)

- Joined YC in 2011 as designer/low-level staffer after Posterous stalled
- Co-founded Initialized Capital in 2012 with Harj Taggar + Alexis Ohanian, $7M Fund I
- Fund I returned 11–14X TVPI driven by Coinbase + Instacart
- **Insight on ownership**: percentage stakes matter more than fund size. Fund II (5X larger, $40M) didn't 5X check sizes — diluted ownership reduced ultimate returns despite multiple unicorns produced

**Rewriting YC's playbook:**
- Maintained constant 2% acceptance rate while scaling 12 → 70+ companies/batch
- "If you select fewer companies, you're reducing innovation rates"
- Introduced batch sharding to manage growth without losing community
- 2011 SV Angel/DST deal (Yuri Milner committed to fund every YC company) transformed seed financing from ad-hoc to systematic

**Influencer investing:**
- Credits PG essays + Hacker News as revolutionary
- Direct founder-investor access > institutional formality
- YouTube crucial for democratizing startup knowledge + attracting talent through authentic storytelling

**Core founder advice:**
- Hire people smarter than yourself relentlessly
- Focus on "Why now?" — timing insight that makes markets viable
- Building startups demands mastery across engineering, design, business simultaneously
- Tan recommends reading PG's Founder Mode essay
