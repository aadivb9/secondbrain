## The Bet Against LLMs

> "LLMs manipulate language really well" — but **lack the capability to truly understand the physical world**.

> The path to superintelligence through scaling LLMs is "complete bullshit."

> "LLMs are now technology development, not research."

> To academics: "Don't work on LLMs. There is no point. You're not going to be able to rival what's going on in industry."

**Prediction:** LLMs obsolete in **5 years**.

## AMI Labs (2026)

- Left Meta November 2025 (12 years as Chief AI Scientist)
- Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs announced March 2026
- **$1.03B seed at $3.5B valuation**
- Paris-headquartered, multi-continent offices
- Positioned as credible frontier AI outside US-China binary

## JEPA — Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture

**Core idea:** Train AI to learn an abstract representation of the world and make predictions in that space, ignoring unpredictable details.

> "Learns underlying rules of the world from observation, like a baby learning about gravity"

- Foundation for common sense + reasoning
- Trains from video, audio, sensor data — not text
- LLMs perform poorly at learning representations of images/video — "they really do not work"

## What AI Should Focus On Instead

- World models for robotics + autonomous systems
- Industrial applications using sensor data from complex processes
- Domestic robotics + Level 5 autonomous driving
- Smart assistants that predict user actions

> "Breakthroughs will emerge from academia pursuing novel techniques rather than refining existing language models."

## Davos 2026 Clash

- LeCun publicly clashed with Dario Amodei + Demis Hassabis over the path to intelligent systems
- Disagreement: scaling LLMs (Amodei/Hassabis to varying degrees) vs new architectures (LeCun)
