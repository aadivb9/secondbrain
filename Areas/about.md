## How I work

Most productivity advice is noise. Here's what actually holds up for me:

**Systems beat willpower.** I'm literally building an app around this premise. Motivation expires. Design doesn't. Default to whatever structure forces the right behavior on autopilot.

**Ship ugly first.** A janky working prototype beats a clean idea. You cannot edit a blank page into a real product.

**Pick one thing and get unreasonably good at it.** Rock Lee had no ninjutsu. He trained taijutsu until it was terrifying. One deep skill beats five shallow ones.

**Speed over perfect.** Hinata couldn't set, couldn't spike properly, couldn't read the game. Made nationals on speed and refusal to stop. The analog applies to CS and to life.

**Isolation is a trap.** Sasuke had everything and nearly destroyed himself. The best engineers I read about ask for help early. Cutting people off isn't strength — it's fear with a costume on.

**Know what you're grinding for.** Solo Leveling's actual thesis: daily discipline compounds, but only when you remember the why. Without the why, the grind eats you.

**Setter brain applies everywhere.** Your job is to make the people around you look good. Do that and you don't have to be the ace.
